⚙️ Assessment Team · Adaptive Learning Strategy

Assessment as the Engine for Personalization

The Assessment team owns the intelligence layer of the learner journey — collecting skill signals at every moment, modeling where each learner is, and determining how the experience adapts to get them to their goal.

🎯
Anchoring Purpose
Every learner has a skill goal. The entire system exists to close the gap between where they are and where they want to be.
Assessment is the mechanism that reads that gap, tracks progress against it, and tells the system how to close it faster — for every learner, continuously.

⚙️ The Assessment Engine

Assessment Team · Core Ownership
▾
01 · Capture
📡
Signal Collection
Reading what every learner interaction tells us

Reads every learner interaction — passive signals during content (engagement, time, behavior) and active signals at evaluation moments (accuracy, patterns, attempts), plus diagnostic baseline and stated learning context. Defines what counts as a meaningful skill signal.

→
02 · Model
🧠
Learner Modeling
Building a dynamic picture of where each learner is

Builds a continuously updated picture of each learner — concept-level skill state, gap identification, and pace & retention patterns — with psychometric rigor (item quality, reliability, validity) behind the data.

→
03 · Adapt
🎯
Adaptation Logic
Translating model insights into personalized next steps

Translates model insights into action — mastery thresholds, remediation triggers, pathway selection for the next block, and the progress signals surfaced back to learners and instructors.

Opportunity #1
Learner Skill Baseline Project: PRD ↗

How might we understand where each learner is before the course begins?

What establishes the learner's baseline

The quality of all downstream personalization depends on getting this baseline right. A well-connected Onboarding and Skill Diagnostic gives us what we need from day one: what each learner already knows, where their gaps are, and where to begin. Everything else — matching, adaptation, progress signals — is measured against this starting point.

Opportunity #2

How might we use practice to close individual skill gaps before learners reach the graded assessment?

How practice is generated

Practice is generated by computing the delta between our most up-to-date model of the learner — what they currently know and where they're still uncertain — and the skills required to pass the Verified Skill Assessment. That gap becomes the input: every practice item is targeted to close it.

As learners work through practice, their signals continuously update the learner model, narrowing the gap in real time and adjusting what comes next. Done well, learners arrive at the Verified Skill Assessment having already worked through their gaps — not discovering them.
